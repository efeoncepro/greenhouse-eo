# Engagement, wellbeing, development + retention

Load when the task touches onboarding, development, engagement, burnout, wellness, or retention. Hiring well is wasted if people disengage and leave — and in 2026 engagement is in crisis.

## The 2026 engagement crisis (data)

- Gallup: **global engagement fell to 20%** (from a 23% peak in 2022) — ~**80% not engaged**, an estimated **$10 trillion** in lost productivity. ([Gallup 2026](https://www.gallup.com/workplace/349484/state-of-the-global-workplace.aspx))
- Wellbeing: only **34% "thriving"** (ticked up slightly). **Manager burnout is accelerating**; **women report more burnout** (46% vs 37%; women leaders 43% vs 31% men). Europe is worst engaged (~73% not engaged).
- **"Quiet quitting"** = not-engaged people present-but-not-performing; combined with stagnation it's a slow bleed.
- **Structured wellbeing programs correlate with 25–40% lower turnover.** ([Speakwise](https://speakwiseapp.com/blog/employee-engagement-statistics), [MangoApps/Gallup](https://www.mangoapps.com/articles/gallup-2026-state-of-the-global-workplace))

**The 2026 insight (Forbes/Gallup): the problem is organizational systems, not people.** Engagement dropped most where **managers** are overloaded and systems are broken — fix the system (manager quality, clarity, workload), not the individual. ([Forbes 2026](https://www.forbes.com/sites/aparnarae/2026/05/13/employee-engagement-hit-a-10-year-low-the-problem-isnt-people---its-organizational-systems/))

## Onboarding + development (retention starts on day 0)

- **Onboarding** is a retention lever, not paperwork: clarity of role (the scorecard again), early wins, a manager 1:1 cadence, and a 30/60/90 plan. In Greenhouse, onboarding is HRIS-owned (TASK-030/770) — talent hands over a clean scorecard + expectations.
- **Ramp to productivity**: define what "fully ramped" means per role and track time-to-ramp.
- **Development**: growth is the top retention driver for Millennials/Gen Z. Career paths, upskilling, stretch assignments, 9-box for talent review, real feedback loops. Manager quality is the multiplier.

## Wellbeing / wellness (real, not fruit-in-the-office)

- **Psychological safety** (Amy Edmondson) is the foundation — can people speak up, admit mistakes, disagree without fear? It predicts learning + performance and is the antidote to quiet quitting.
- **Workload + burnout**: burnout is mostly a *systems* problem (chronic overload, unclear priorities, poor manager support), not a resilience deficit. Monitor load — and in Greenhouse, **ICO delivery load / over-allocation is a real burnout/flight-risk signal** (see `workforce-planning.md`).
- **Real wellness** = manageable workload + autonomy + fair pay + recognition + flexibility + genuine mental-health support. Perks don't fix a broken system.
- **Manager burnout** is the accelerating edge — protect and enable managers first; they're the transmission for everyone else's engagement.

## Retention + flight risk

- **Leading signals**: engagement drop, disengagement in 1:1s, over-allocation/overwork, missed recognition, stalled growth, comp drift vs market (pay transparency makes this visible fast), manager change.
- **eNPS + pulse surveys**: measure, but act — surveying without action lowers trust.
- **Stay interviews** > exit interviews: ask *before* they're leaving what would keep them.
- **The manager is the #1 retention lever** across every generation — invest there.

## How to apply in Greenhouse

- Treat engagement/wellbeing as a **people-ops discipline with signals**, not vibes. Where a signal exists in the runtime (ICO load, capacity), consume it; where it doesn't, recommend the measurement.
- Keep it **systemic**: recommend fixing manager load, role clarity, and workload before individual "resilience" interventions.
- Boundary: comp/market numbers → finance/payroll; this skill owns the *people* diagnosis + intervention design.
