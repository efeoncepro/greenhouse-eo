# Layout Design & Finishing Pilot — visual scorecard

## Verdict

**PASS — 47/50.** The three formats are release-ready as a digital creative pilot. They preserve the campaign anchor while introducing a more governed editorial system than the original overlay set.

The improvement is not attributed to a model alone. Most of the perceived premium gain comes from the deterministic layout system: one alignment axis, ratio-specific hierarchy, optical scrim, frequency rail, protected negative space and a deliberate closure with URL. Seedream Pro added a restrained finishing delta to the clean plates.

## Format review

### 16:9

- Strongest balance of scale and negative space.
- The left editorial block and rightward bird create a direct Z path.
- The support line and URL no longer float independently.

### 4:5

- Headline sits above the wing line instead of competing with the subject.
- The descending wake activates the lower half without contaminating the copy field.
- Seedream reduced average chroma by 5.5%; visually this reads as cohesion rather than dullness and remains accepted.

### 9:16

- Logo, copy and URL remain outside the declared story interface zones.
- The subject occupies the middle/lower action field while the headline remains fully independent.
- The metaphor survives the narrow format without shrinking into a decorative thumbnail.

## Remaining human gate

Before paid media activation, a creative director should approve the exact copy and campaign objective. A flagship print or OOH adaptation would still require manual retouch at 100%, vendor dimensions and ICC proofing; this pilot covers digital static only.
