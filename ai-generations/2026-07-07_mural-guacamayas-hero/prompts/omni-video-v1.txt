Create a 10-second vertical 9:16 cinematic social media reel.

Use the reference images as the visual storyboard, in this order:
1. Start from the close-up eye painted on the wall: a hand-painted macaw mural, wet concrete, thick acrylic paint, moody amber and teal light.
2. Transition into the wing breaking out of the wall: the mural surface becomes liquid, paint stretches into glossy strings, feathers remain half painted and half alive.
3. Briefly reach the energy of the flying paint-trail macaws, but keep the style artistic and mural-like, not wildlife documentary.
4. End by returning toward the painted macaw eye or a similar close mural detail so the clip can loop.

Core idea: "The mural comes alive. Color refuses to stay still."

Motion direction:
- First second: almost still, tense macro shot; the painted eye subtly blinks or catches a living reflection.
- Seconds 1-3: slow push-out reveals the urban mural on a dark wet alley wall; tiny paint droplets move upward.
- Seconds 3-6: a large blue and green macaw wing pushes through the wall as if concrete became water; thick acrylic paint peels, stretches, and turns into living feathers.
- Seconds 6-8.5: one or two macaws made of living feathers and liquid brush strokes fly upward through the alley, leaving cobalt blue and emerald green paint trails.
- Seconds 8.5-10: the flying color folds back into mural texture, landing on a painted eye for a clean loop.

Camera and look:
- Cinematic macro-to-wide progression, slow push-in/push-out, subtle handheld realism, layered depth.
- Premium street art campaign, real wet concrete, torn posters, old stickers, rain marks, paint texture, film grain.
- Cobalt blue and emerald green dominate; minimal yellow accents; avoid red carnival colors.
- Golden rim light with teal shadow, deep contrast, high-end editorial grade.

Hard constraints:
- No people.
- No text.
- No logos.
- No captions.
- No watermark or visible signature.
- Do not make it cute, cartoonish, touristy, or documentary.
- Keep the bird visually hybrid: part mural, part living paint, part feathers.
